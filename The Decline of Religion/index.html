Key Information about the Book/Essay/Play

  • Book/Essay/Play: The Decline of Religion
  • Author: C.S Lewis
  • Written In: 1946
  • Prominent Theme(s) in the Book/Essay/Play: Religious Devotion
  • Brief Description: C.S. Lewis points to the diminishing societal pressure as the reason for the decline in church attendance. He paints this in a positive light, claiming that this change separates the lukewarm Christians from the true Christians.
  • Similar Books/Essays/Plays: Orthodoxy
  • Books/Essays/Plays by the Same Author: Screwtape Letters, The Chronicles of Narnia, Mere Christianity

"If the various anticlerical and antitheistic forces at work in the nineteenth century had had to attack a solid phalanx of radical Christians the story might have been different. But mere "religion"—"morality tinged with emotion," "what a man does with his solitude," "the religion of all good men"—has little power of resistance. It is not good at saying No."

Theme(s): Religious Devotion

"But later on, as the real meaning of the Christian claim becomes apparent, its demand for total surrender, the sheer chasm between Nature and Supernature, men are increasingly "offended." Dislike, terror, and finally hatred succeed: none who will not give it what it asks (and it asks all) can endure it: all who are not with it are against it."
